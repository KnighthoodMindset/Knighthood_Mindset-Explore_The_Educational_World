Below are the most common approaches: #### 1. **Stereo Vision** * Mimics human binocular vision. * Two or more cameras capture the scene from different viewpoints. * By comparing corresponding pixels dense reconstruction. #### 3. **Photometric Stereo** * Uses multiple images taken from the same viewpoint but under different lighting conditions. * Analyzes variations in shading to infer surface normals and depth. #### 4. **Laser Scanning (LiDAR)** * Emits laser pulses and measures the time taken for them to return (Time-of-Flight). * Generates highly accurate 3D point clouds, useful in surveying and autonomous navigation. #### 5. **Depth Sensors** * Devices like Microsoft Kinect use structured light or time-of-flight technology to directly capture depth information. * Combines RGB and depth data to generate 3D representations in real time. #### 6. **Volumetric Reconstruction** * Combines depth information over time to create voxel-based models. * Common in medical imaging (e.g., CT and MRI scans). --- ### üß¨ **Characteristics of 3D Reconstruction** | Feature | Description | | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | |
