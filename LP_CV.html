Oriented Gradients (HOG) + SVM** * One of the earliest and most popular approaches. * The image is divided into small cells, and for each cell, a histogram of edge directions is calculated. * These histograms form a descriptor, which is then fed to a Support Vector Machine (SVM) classifier to detect pedestrians. * **Advantages**: Robust to background noise and lighting changes. * **Disadvantages**: Computationally intensive and may not work well with occluded or small pedestrians. --- ### 2. **Deep Learning Approaches (CNNs)** With the advent of deep learning, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have significantly improved pedestrian detection accuracy. #### a. **R-CNN, Fast R-CNN, and Faster R-CNN** * Region-based methods that propose object regions and classify them using a CNN. * Faster R-CNN is real-time and more efficient, widely used in self-driving cars. #### b. **YOLO (You Only Look Once) and SSD (Single Shot Multibox Detector)** * These methods are single-stage detectors, meaning they detect objects in one pass through the network. * They are significantly faster than R-CNN variants, suitable for real-time pedestrian detection. #### c. **Transformer-based Models (e.g., DETR)** * Recently, transformer models have been applied for object detection, including pedestrian detection. * They can model global context better than CNNs alone, improving detection under occlusions. --- ### 3. **Infrared and Thermal Imaging** * Used especially in low-light or nighttime conditions where visible light fails. * Pedestrians emit heat, which thermal sensors can capture. * Often combined with visible spectrum imaging to improve detection accuracy. --- ### 4. **Stereo Vision and Depth Sensors** * Using multiple cameras to estimate depth allows better separation of pedestrians from background objects. * LIDAR and stereo cameras can measure distance to the object, which helps in locating pedestrians in 3D space. --- ### 5. **Sensor Fusion** * Combining inputs from cameras, LIDAR, radar, and other sensors improves robustness and accuracy. * Used in autonomous vehicles and robotics where reliability is critical. --- ##
