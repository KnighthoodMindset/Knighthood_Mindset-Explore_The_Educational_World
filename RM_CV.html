In computer vision and intelligent transport systems (ITS), recognizing road markings plays a significant role in enabling autonomous driving, traffic monitoring, and road safety systems. --- ### üéØ Objectives of Road Markings: * **Lane demarcation:** Separate traffic lanes. * **Direction indication:** Guide turns, overtaking zones, and crossings. * **Speed control:** Highlight speed zones, pedestrian crossings, or school areas. * **Safety assurance:** Warn about hazards, no-entry zones, or sharp turns. --- ### üîç Types of Road Markings: 1. **Longitudinal Markings:** * Run parallel to the direction of traffic. * Examples: Lane lines, center lines, edge lines. * Solid lines restrict crossing; dashed lines permit crossing. 2. **Transverse Markings:** * Placed perpendicular to the traffic flow. * Examples: Stop lines, pedestrian crossings (zebra crossings), give-way lines. 3. **Special Markings:** * Arrows for directional movement. * Text (like "STOP", "SLOW"). * Speed limit numbers. * Box junctions and cycle boxes. 4. **Colored Markings:** * Yellow or white: Regular markings. * Red: No-stopping zones. * Blue: Reserved zones (e.g., for disabled parking). --- ### üí° Computer Vision Techniques for Detecting Road Markings 1. **Image Preprocessing:** * **Noise reduction** using filters like Gaussian blur. * **Color space transformation** to grayscale or HSV for contrast enhancement. 2. **Edge Detection:** * Techniques like **Sobel**, **Canny**, or **Laplacian** operators are used to highlight edges of markings. 3. **Thresholding and Segmentation:** * Used to isolate bright road markings from darker pavement. * **Adaptive thresholding** for dynamic environments. 4. **Hough Transform:** * Used for detecting straight lines (e.g., lane lines). * Effective in structured environments like highways. 5. **Morphological Operations:** * **Erosion/Dilation** to remove small artifacts and enhance line continuity. 6. **Deep Learning:** * Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are trained to detect complex patterns like arrows or textual signs. * Semantic segmentation models like U-Net or DeepLab are used for pixel-wise classification. --- ### üß†
